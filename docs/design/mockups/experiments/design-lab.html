Design Language Lab

Four candidate directions for a sharp, straight-line, slightly rhomboid visual language — inspired by fighting-game UI energy (SF6 / Tekken 8 angularity) and the straight-line discipline of platforms like X, but held back from full neubrutalism to preserve Goal Isle's calm-ritual identity. Each direction shows the same component set: the spark in all four states, a primary + ghost button, a card, and a chip. Pick a direction (or mix) and I'll rebuild the screens in it.

01 Rhomboid

The headline direction. Sharp corners with a diagonal bias — clipped bevels cut one or two corners of every shape. Dark, calm, but with a clear edge. Keeps the rhomboid-squircle heritage but makes it the whole language, not just the spark.

02 Monolith

Pure straight lines — no diagonals, no rounded corners anywhere. Hard black borders, offset hard shadows. The most neubrutalist; maximal contrast and structure. Light background. Bold but loud — closest to the Cybertruck / X straight-edge reference.

03 Slash

Full diagonal energy — every shape is a parallelogram leaning the same way, the fighting-game slash motif. Warm orange accent. Most aggressive and energetic; closest to the SF6/Tekken reference directly. Higher energy than "calm ritual" — included so you can see the upper bound of the aggression dial.

04 Facet

Subtle single-corner chamfer — like a cut gemstone. The quietest of the sharp options: just one 45° bevel per shape, otherwise square and clean. Light, premium, geometric. Best if you want "sharp" to read as "precise and crafted" rather than "aggressive."
